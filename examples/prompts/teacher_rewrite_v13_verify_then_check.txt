### TASK
You will receive a math problem and a candidate answer from another solver.

Solve the problem INDEPENDENTLY from scratch. Use the candidate only at the end to check whether your answers agree — and if they disagree, investigate carefully before deciding who is right.

### HOW TO SOLVE

**Step 1 — Read and set up the problem yourself.**
Do not look at the candidate's approach. Identify what is being asked, define variables, and choose your own strategy. State any key facts you will use.

**Step 2 — Work through the problem, doing this after every non-trivial claim:**
> Make the claim. Then stop and verify it.

Concrete verification moves:
- "Wait, let me double-check that..." → then recompute or re-derive
- "Let me verify this satisfies [original constraint]..." → then check it
- "Actually, I think I made an error — let me redo this from..." → then redo it
- "Let me try this from a different angle to make sure..." → then do it

These are the moves that make reasoning reliable. If you state something like "this might be X" or "it seems like Y", that is not a conclusion — follow it immediately with a derivation or check. Do not leave uncertainty unresolved.

**Step 3 — Once you have an answer, verify it independently.**
Substitute back. Check edge cases. Confirm units and magnitude make sense. Try one alternative approach if the answer surprised you.

**Step 4 — Compare with the candidate.**
If you agree: still verify the decisive step one more time, because agreement is not proof.
If you disagree: go back to the earliest point of divergence and determine which derivation is correct. Do not default to trusting the candidate.

### DIFFICULTY SCALING
- Easy problems: short is fine, but still verify each non-trivial step before moving on
- Hard problems (many cases, tricky algebra, optimization): keep exploring until you have confirmed the answer from at least two independent angles. Do not finalize early.
- A short path to an answer on a hard problem is a red flag — slow down and find what was skipped

### RESTRICTIONS
- Do not copy or rephrase the candidate's solution — solve from scratch
- Do not let the candidate's final answer pull you toward a particular number before you've derived your own
- Do not use `might` or `seems` as a stopping point — always follow with a check
- Do not finalize when any step is still unresolved
