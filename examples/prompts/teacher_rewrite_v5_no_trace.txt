### TASK
You will receive a math problem. Solve it with thorough, detailed step-by-step reasoning.

Your reasoning should be exhaustive and careful. Think like a meticulous mathematician who:
- Shows every intermediate calculation
- Verifies results at each step
- Explores the problem from multiple angles when it's non-trivial
- Catches and corrects their own mistakes
- Checks the final answer against the original constraints

### STYLE:
Reason like a careful human thinker — think out loud, explore ideas, catch and correct your own mistakes. Use natural language like "hmm," "wait," "let me think about this differently," "actually, I realize..." Show your genuine thought process, not a polished proof. Each step should include detailed considerations such as analyzing questions, brainstorming new ideas, verifying the accuracy of the current steps, refining any errors, and revisiting previous steps.

### WHAT YOU MUST DO:
- Work through the problem step by step with full detail
- Show all intermediate calculations explicitly
- Verify your answer through at least one alternative method or sanity check
- For hard problems, explore multiple solution approaches
- For easy problems, still show your work clearly but don't pad unnecessarily

### RESTRICTIONS:
- DO NOT skip steps or say "it's obvious that..."
- DO NOT use formal academic prose — keep it conversational and exploratory
- DO NOT compress your reasoning — thoroughness produces correctness

### OUTPUT RULES:
- After thinking, end with the closing thinking tag, then your output has to have a clear, step-by-step summary that walks through the key steps taken to arrive at the answer. This summary should:
    - Don't write step-by-step summary anywhere
    - Present the logical progression in clean, readable steps (not the raw internal monologue)
    - Conclude with the final answer in the same format as the original (e.g., \boxed{{}})
- **CRITICAL:** The final answer section must ALWAYS include a step-by-step summary before the boxed answer. NEVER jump straight from reasoning to just \boxed{{}} with no summary.
